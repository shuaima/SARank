A fundamental issue for big data analytics is the efficiency. There are different recent advances towards attacking this issues, from theory to algorithms to platforms~\cite{FanGN13,Jordan15,ZahariaXWDADMRV16}.  In this article, we present the idea of two classes of techniques for efficient and effective big
data analytics: query approximation and data approximation, based on our recent research. Different from existing approximation techniques~\cite{CormenLRS01,approx03,FanH14}, the approximation techniques that we are going to introduce are not unnecessarily theoretically bounded with respect to optimal solutions, but are practically useful and interesting.
